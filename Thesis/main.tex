\begin{abstract}
	In this work we study the phenomena of superradiance in the context of General Relativity, with particular focus on scaterring of electromagnetic waves by rotating black hole.
	We give a short introduction to Kerr black holes, specifying some of their symmetries and properties.
	We then introduce the Newman-Penrose formalism to study wave perturbations in a Kerr background, and obtain the electromagnetic case of Teukolsky's master equation.
	This equation is both solved using approximate analitycal methods and numerical techniques that are presented in detail.
	We obtain the amplification/absorption factor for each multipole mode with high precision, obtaining a very good agreement with those found in the literature.
	Finally, we discuss the scattering of realistic electromagnetic waves by a Kerr BH, which are superpositions of these different modes.
\end{abstract}
